The Problem

Current Shopping List apps on the market serve their purpose as a record keeping and organization tool but do little to assist customers once they've actually entered the store and began their venture for their items. When considering the constant backtracking, searching, and overall frustration that goes into filling your shopping cart, the customer is more or less forced to memorize a store's layout and feel more inclined to stick with that certain location or grocery outlet, rather than frequent unfamiliar but perhaps more convenient locations throughout their daily life. These shopping lists contain a bulk of untapped information and detail about items that with the right amount of tweaking could be shared and made readily available to other users, maintaining a consistent record of their location (as well as other details) throughout a stores layout, bringing customers out of the dark and into the light about the best method to tackle their list and allowing a public and crowd-sourced database of local grocers' product information. A rapid fire UI system for item entry means no time wasted for the user building his catologue, and paves the way for future efficiency in shopping such as shortest path finding for your list and more.
